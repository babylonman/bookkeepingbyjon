Privacy Policy

Effective Date: December 14, 2025

Last Updated: December 14, 2025

Introduction

Bookkeeping by Jon ("we," "us," "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our QuickBooks Online integration tools and related software (the "Application").

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By using the Application, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

Key Privacy Commitment: We do not store your QuickBooks financial data on our servers. All data processing happens locally on your machine.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

When you use the Application, you may provide:

  • QuickBooks Credentials: OAuth 2.0 tokens to access your QuickBooks Online account
  • Configuration Data: Settings and preferences for the Application
  • Source Files: Shopify and Amazon export files you choose to upload for validation

1.2 Information Automatically Collected

The Application may collect:

  • Usage Information: Which features you use, frequency of use
  • Error Logs: Technical errors and debugging information (stored locally)
  • System Information: Operating system version, Python version

1.3 Information from QuickBooks Online

Through the QuickBooks API, the Application accesses:

  • Chart of accounts
  • Transactions (deposits, journal entries, invoices, etc.)
  • Customer and vendor information
  • Items and products
  • Company information

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide Services: Connect to QuickBooks, extract data, validate transactions, and import missing data
  • Process Locally: All data validation and reconciliation occurs on your local machine
  • Generate Reports: Create validation reports and export files for your review
  • Authenticate: Manage OAuth 2.0 authentication with QuickBooks Online
  • Improve Application: Understand usage patterns to improve features (anonymized only)
  • Provide Support: Respond to your questions and technical issues

3. How We Store Your Information

3.1 Local Storage Only

Financial Data: Your QuickBooks financial data is NEVER sent to our servers. All processing happens locally on your machine.

3.2 What We Store Locally

The Application stores the following on your local machine:

  • OAuth Tokens: Stored in your .env file (encrypted)
  • Exported Data: CSV and JSON files in your local directories
  • Configuration: Application settings and preferences
  • Logs: Error and debugging logs (if enabled)

3.3 What We May Store on Our Servers

We may collect and store (anonymously):

  • Application version numbers
  • Error reports (without financial data)
  • Aggregated usage statistics

4. How We Share Your Information

4.1 We Do Not Sell Your Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information or financial data to third parties.

4.2 Third-Party Services

The Application integrates with:

  • QuickBooks Online (Intuit): Your data is transmitted directly to QuickBooks via their official API. See Intuit's Privacy Policy.
  • GitHub: Our code is hosted on GitHub. No financial data is stored in our repositories.

4.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, such as to comply with a subpoena or similar legal process, or when we believe disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others.

5. Data Security

5.1 Security Measures

We implement security measures including:

  • OAuth 2.0: Industry-standard authentication protocol
  • HTTPS: Encrypted communication with QuickBooks API
  • Local Processing: Financial data never leaves your machine
  • Token Encryption: Access tokens stored securely
  • No Server Storage: We don't store your financial data on servers

5.2 Your Responsibilities

You are responsible for:

  • Keeping your .env file secure and private
  • Not sharing your OAuth tokens or credentials
  • Securing your local machine where data is stored
  • Revoking application access if you suspect unauthorized use

6. Your Privacy Rights

6.1 Access and Control

You have the right to:

  • Access: View all data stored locally by the Application
  • Delete: Remove all local data by deleting the Application and its files
  • Revoke Access: Disconnect the Application from QuickBooks at any time
  • Export: All data exports are already in standard formats (CSV, JSON)

6.2 Revoking Access

To revoke the Application's access to QuickBooks:

  1. Log in to your QuickBooks Online account
  2. Go to Settings → Manage your Intuit Account → Sign in & Security → Apps
  3. Find "Bookkeeping by Jon" and click "Disconnect"

6.3 California Privacy Rights

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to request information about the personal information we collect and how we use it.

7. Data Retention

Since all financial data is stored locally on your machine, you control retention periods. To delete all data:

  1. Delete the Application directory
  2. Delete your .env file containing OAuth tokens
  3. Delete any exported CSV/JSON files
  4. Revoke QuickBooks access as described above

8. Children's Privacy

The Application is not intended for use by anyone under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us.

9. International Data Transfers

10. Third-Party Links
